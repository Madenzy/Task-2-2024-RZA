Meet Our Animals

Explore our diverse collection of animals from around the world — from powerful predators to gentle giants and playful creatures. Each one has a unique story and natural habitat.

Lion

Savannah

Africa

Known as the “king of the jungle”, lions live in groups called prides.

Fun fact: A lion’s roar can travel up to 8 km!

Elephant

Grasslands

Africa & Asia

The largest land mammal with strong memory and social behavior.

Fun fact: Elephants can recognize themselves in mirrors.

Giraffe

Savannah

Africa

Tallest land animal with a long neck used for feeding on treetops.

Fun fact: Their tongues can reach 45 cm in length!

Penguin

Coastal

Southern Hemisphere

Flightless birds that “fly” through water using their flippers.

Fun fact: Penguins mate for life!

Red Panda

Forest

Himalayas

Cute, tree-loving animals often mistaken for raccoons.

Fun fact: They communicate with a “twittering” sound.

Meerkat

Desert

Southern Africa

Small mammals known for standing upright while keeping watch.

Fun fact: Meerkats live in groups called mobs.

Tiger

Jungle

Asia

Largest big cat species with powerful strength and stealth.

Fun fact: No two tigers have the same stripe pattern.

Zebra

Savannah

Africa

Known for their black and white stripes, each zebra is unique.

Fun fact: Stripes help confuse predators.

Kangaroo

Outback

Australia

Strong jumpers that carry their babies (joeys) in a pouch.

Fun fact: Kangaroos can’t walk backwards.

Flamingo

Wetlands

Africa, Asia, Americas

Colourful birds known for standing on one leg.

Fun fact: Their pink colour comes from the food they eat!
